INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup (227 g) butter
  • 2 cups (250 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (215 g) soft brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup (73 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs (100 g)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1½ cups (216 g) dark chocolate

METHOD

  1. Cook butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat, stirring often, until it foams,
    then browns, 5-8 minutes. Scrape into a large bowl until cool enough to touch.

  2. Meanwhile, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl.

  3. Add brown sugar and granulated sugar to browned butter. Using an electric mixer on
    medium speed, beat until incorporated. Add eggs and vanilla, and beat until mixture
    lightens and begins to thicken.Reduce mixer speed to low; add dry ingredients and
    fold just to combine. Mix in chocolate pieces with a wooden spoon or spatula.Let mixture
    sit in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to allow flour to hydrate. Dough will look very
    loose at first, but will thicken as it sits.

  4. Place a rack in middle of oven; preheat to 190°c. Using a ice cream scoop, portion out balls of
    dough and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing about 10cm apart (you can also
    form dough into balls with your hands). Do not flatten; cookies will spread as they bake.

  5. Bake cookies until edges are golden brown and firm but centers are still soft, 9-11 minutes.
    Let cool on baking sheets 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and let cool completely.
